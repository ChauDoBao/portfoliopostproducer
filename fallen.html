FALLEN

CG Artist, VFX, Compositor

“Fallen” is an experimental live-action VFX project that explores the destruction of nature caused by human expansion, embodied through the figure of an “angel of nature.” This ethereal being becomes increasingly wounded and diminished as humanity progresses through three key eras: the Agricultural Revolution, the Industrial Revolution, and the Modern Age. Visually and emotionally, the piece evokes a sense of mythology and melancholy: blending magical realism with fragile, dreamlike imagery to express the tension between creation and decay.

TECHNICAL PROCESS

3D Assets & Simulations

To achieve the project's ethereal aesthetic, I utilized Blender 5.0 and Geometry Nodes. The "plant of life" (Lily) was built procedurally, instancing petals and stamens along curves to animate organic growth using Trim Curve parameters. For the environment, I employed the Scatter-on-surface modifier to layer vegetation, while ensuring the "Heart of Nature" model maintained clean topology for deformation.

Simulations: I created fluid simulations for fairy dust, water, and atmospheric fog. While I tested Blender for ground fog, I integrated Embergen for complex fire and smoke simulations to achieve better interaction and visual fidelity.

Compositing & Color Grading

The compositing pipeline was executed in Nuke, focusing on seamlessly integrating CG elements with live-action footage. My workflow involved:

  • Keying: Utilized Primatte for hard mattes and Keylight/IBK for soft matte refinement on hair and transparent fabrics.
  • Integration: Applied Lightwrap nodes to match background lighting and Exponential Glow for physical realism.
  • Color Management: Implemented an ACES workflow with Color Space Transforms (CST) to maintain industry standards.
  • LookDev: Thanks to our colorist Anh Tho, we finalized the look with Halation and Grain effects to emulate a cinematic film texture.
