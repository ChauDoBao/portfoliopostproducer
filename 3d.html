HANOIAN STREET BOY

3D Designer

This individual project followed the theme of “Vietnamese Anti-hero”. As an anti-hero can be defined as a main character who may lack some conventional heroic qualities and attributes, I decided to create a character based on the concept of a Hanoian “Street Boy” (which is called “Boy Phố” in Vietnamese). The reason why this character is a reasonable choice for the theme of a “Vietnamese anti-hero” is that:

In Vietnam, the term “street boys” is typically associated with a negative kind of rebellious adolescent, who does not have the usual characteristics of a hero. They tend to smoke, race and often do not focus on studying.

Due to some insights from frequent Internet users, street boys exist in many cultures, but their appearance differs from region to region. Particularly, street boys in Hanoi are distinctive from other places in Vietnam and easy to recognize.

VISUAL REFERENCES

Gia Huy (he/him), 18 years old, Hanoi, Vietnam

Appearance: He has the typical look of a Hanoian street boy: an edgy appearance with a mismatched outfit. However, unlike a normal human, he has eagle wings on his back.

Background: Huy grew up in the bustling streets of Hanoi. Being raised in a dysfunctional family with a frail mother and an alcoholic abusive dad, he often witnessed his mom being beaten, and he himself was also a victim of domestic violence. Going through these kinds of trauma forced him to portray a more edgy image. When his desperation to fight against his abusive father reached a climax, he was granted special powers to save himself and his mother, as well as other people with similar hardship out there.

Ability
Fly: He uses his eagle wings to go to victims’ places faster (no traffic jam).
Hypnotize: Using his right eye, he can hypnotize the abuser and make them change their personality.
Boar spear: He uses this weapon because he thinks it will make him look cooler and scare his opponents.
